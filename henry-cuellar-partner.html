    Henry Cuellar Partner: Who Is Imelda Cuellar?: Henry Cuellar, also known as Enrique Roberto “Henry” Cuellar was born on September 19, 1955.

    He’s an American attorney and politician serving as the U.S. representative for Texas’s 28th congressional district since 2005. Cuellar is a member of the Democratic Party.

    Henry Cuellar Partner: Who Is Imelda Cuellar?

    Henry Cuellar is married to Imelda Cuellar. It’s unclear when they met and when they tied the knot. Also, owing to the fact that Imelda lives away from the spotlight, we do not have any information regarding her date of birth, age, and occupation at the time of this report.

    Cuellar’s district extends from the Rio Grande to San Antonio’s suburbs. He served in the Texas House of Representatives for 14 years, from 1987 to 2001

    Cuellar briefly served as the Texas Secretary of State in 2001, making him, as of 2022, the most recent Democrat to have held a statewide office in Texas.

    Cuellar was first elected to Congress in 2004 after defeating Ciro Rodriguez. He’s considered a leading centrist within the House Democratic Caucus.

    Cuellar has since been reelected in every general election while surviving a number of competitive primary races, most notably in 2020 and 2022.

    In October 2023, Cuellar hit significant headlines after he was reportedly carjacked by three armed robbers on Monday 2, October.

    In a statement, Cuellar’s chief of staff, Jacob Hochberg said: “As Congressman Cuellar was parking his car this evening, 3 armed assailants approached the Congressman and stole his vehicle. Luckily, he was not harmed and is working with local law enforcement”

    Hochberg said police recovered Cuellar’s vehicle. According to reports, the robbery happened in Washington’s Navy Yard area, about a mile from the U.S. Capitol.

    Monday’s carjacking happens to be the second assault on a member of Congress in the District of Columbia this year (2023).

    In February, Angie Craig was assaulted in her apartment, suffering bruises while escaping serious injury. Her chief of staff said the attack did not appear to be politically motivated.
